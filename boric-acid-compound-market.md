<p><p>The Boric Acid Compound market includes two primary types: Anhydrous Borate and Borate with Water. Anhydrous Borate refers to boric acid in its pure, water-free form, often used in industrial applications like glass manufacturing, ceramics, and flame retardants due to its high stability and reactivity. Borate with Water, or hydrated boric acid, contains water molecules and is commonly utilized in agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and personal care products for its solubility and mild antiseptic properties. Both types cater to diverse industries, driven by their unique chemical properties.</p></p>
