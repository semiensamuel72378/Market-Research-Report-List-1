<p><p>PET nuclear medicine imaging is a non-invasive diagnostic technique that utilizes positron emission tomography (PET) to visualize metabolic processes in the body. This innovative imaging modality offers detailed images of how tissues and organs function, making it particularly valuable in oncology, cardiology, and neurology. The PET imaging process involves the administration of radiotracers, which emit positrons and are tracked by a PET scanner to produce high-resolution images.</p><p>The PET Nuclear Medicine Imaging Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.4% during the forecast period. The market is also seeing trends toward the development of novel radiotracers and hybrid imaging systems that combine PET with CT or MRI for improved clinical outcomes.
