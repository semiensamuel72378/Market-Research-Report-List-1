<p><p>The Hepatitis B Immune Globulin (HBIG) market is primarily segmented into two types based on administration routes: intravenous injection and intramuscular injection. Intravenous injection delivers the immune globulin directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid action and higher bioavailability, often used in severe cases or post-exposure situations. In contrast, intramuscular injection involves administering the globulin in muscle tissue, offering a slower release and is commonly used for prophylaxis in less critical scenarios. Each method caters to specific clinical needs and patient conditions.</p></p>
