<p><p>The scleroderma drug market comprises three primary types: anti-inflammatory agents, immunosuppressive agents, and anti-fibrotic agents. Anti-inflammatory agents help reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms associated with scleroderma. Immunosuppressive agents work to dampen the immune response, preventing further tissue damage and progression of the disease. Anti-fibrotic agents specifically target fibrosis, aiming to reduce excessive collagen buildup and restore tissue function. Together, these categories address the multifaceted challenges of managing scleroderma and improving patients' quality of life.</p></p>
