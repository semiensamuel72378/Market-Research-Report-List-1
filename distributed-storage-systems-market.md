<p><p>Distributed storage systems are classified into two main market types: on-premise and cloud-based. On-premise systems involve local data storage within an organization's infrastructure, providing greater control and security but requiring significant initial investment and maintenance. Cloud-based storage, on the other hand, offers scalability and flexibility by hosting data on remote servers, accessible via the internet. This model minimizes hardware costs and allows businesses to pay for resources on a usage basis, making it ideal for dynamic data requirements.</p></p>
