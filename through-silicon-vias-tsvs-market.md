<p><p>Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs) are vertical interconnects that enable communication between different layers of semiconductor chips, enhancing performance and reducing footprint. The market is primarily divided into two types: 2.5D TSVs, which utilize a silicon interposer to connect multiple chips side-by-side, improving thermal management and power efficiency; and 3D TSVs, which stack chips vertically, offering higher integration and better bandwidth. Both types support advanced packaging in applications like high-performance computing and mobile devices.</p></p>
